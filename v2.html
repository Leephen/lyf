Horn theft

A musical instrument store was robbed Saturday night. The thieves broke into the store by smashing a window through one of its doors. He broke open three money boxes and stole 1225 kron. he also took a loudspeaker worth 14,000 kronfrom a shop window and put it in an ordinary loudspeaker case and stole it.

The police examined the scene carefully and concluded that the robbery was the work of someone familiar with the music store. The police narrowed their suspicions to three young apprentices, Hanson, Legg and Headley, and decided that one of them must be the culprit.

The three boys were brought before Mr. Sorensen, the police officer, with three pens and three pieces of paper on the table. Sorensen told them, "I invited you here to ask you to cooperate with me and help me find the criminals. Now you write an essay. You pretend that you are a thief. Then you try to break into the shop and steal something. Okay, let's go. I'll roll in 30 minutes."

After half an hour, Sorensen asked them to stop writing and read their essay.
